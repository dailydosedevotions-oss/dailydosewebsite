Blessed Are: The Merciful - Part 5

Releases Sunday Sep 20, 2026 at 7:00PM

Matthew 5:7 (ESV)

“Blessed are the merciful, for they shall receive mercy.”

Mercy is one of the clearest displays of God's heart, but it is also one of the hardest things for human nature to live out consistently. Most people understand mercy when they are the ones who need patience, forgiveness, understanding, or another chance. It becomes more difficult when we are the ones being asked to show mercy.

Mercy does not mean sin is unimportant. It does not remove wisdom, accountability, justice, or boundaries. Biblical mercy sees wrong honestly, but refuses to respond with cruelty, revenge, bitterness, or a hardened heart.

Joseph's life gives a powerful example. He was betrayed, sold, falsely accused, and imprisoned. Yet when he had power to take revenge, he responded differently. Genesis 50:20 (ESV) says, “As for you, you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good.”

Jesus shows mercy even more fully. While He was being mocked, rejected, tortured, and crucified, He prayed for those responsible. Luke 23:34 (ESV) says, “Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do.”

Mercy can be costly because it requires surrendering pride, bitterness, rage, and the constant desire to replay the offence in the heart. But mercy frees the heart from the prison of resentment.

When someone remembers how deeply they have been forgiven, it changes the way they see others. They become slower to condemn, slower to destroy, and quicker to remember grace.
