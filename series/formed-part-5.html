Daily Dose: FORMED

Part 5 — Work: Serving Faithfully Where God Has Placed You

Releases Sunday Jul 5, 2026 at 7:00PM

Colossians 3:23-24 (ESV)

“Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men, knowing that from the Lord you will receive the inheritance as your reward. You are serving the Lord Christ.”

Work is something every person will spend a large portion of their life doing, yet many people struggle to understand how deeply it connects to spiritual formation. Some people begin worshipping work and build their entire identity around achievement, money, or success. Others drift into laziness, inconsistency, and avoiding responsibility altogether.

But biblically, work was never meant to become an idol or something to escape from.

Work is stewardship.

It is one of the ways people reflect responsibility, faithfulness, consistency, and service within everyday life.

When Paul writes:

“Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men…”

he shifts the focus completely. Work is no longer only about impressing people, gaining status, or proving worth. It becomes something deeper. Even ordinary work can become an act of faithfulness before God when it is done with integrity and humility.

This matters because many people only want the visible results while resisting the process required to build anything meaningful. But throughout life, God often shapes people through ordinary responsibilities long before greater opportunities ever arrive.

I know in my own life there were years where addiction, chaos, and destructive choices slowly destroyed structure, responsibility, and consistency. When your life becomes controlled by escape, showing up faithfully for anything becomes difficult. Purpose slowly fades and survival becomes the focus instead.

And honestly, rebuilding those things does not happen overnight.

There are seasons where simply learning to consistently show up again becomes part of the healing process. Learning responsibility again. Learning structure again. Learning how to remain faithful in small things when everything in you once lived only for immediate relief.

That is why work matters spiritually more than many people realise.

Not because work saves anyone. Not because productivity makes someone valuable. But because faithfulness in ordinary things often becomes part of how God reshapes a person over time.

Proverbs 14:23 (ESV)

“In all toil there is profit, but mere talk tends only to poverty.”

There is a difference between constantly talking about change and quietly building a life through consistent action. Real growth usually looks less dramatic than people imagine. Often it is found in ordinary unseen faithfulness:

getting up when you do not feel motivated

serving well when nobody notices

remaining honest when compromise would be easier

continuing to work with integrity even when tired

This does not mean people should build their identity around work. That becomes dangerous too. Some people stay constantly busy because silence exposes deeper issues they are trying to avoid. Others chase achievement hoping it will finally make them feel valuable enough.

But identity was never meant to be rooted in performance.

Identity is found in Christ.

Work simply becomes one of the places where faithfulness is lived out practically.

Jesus Himself spent years living an ordinary working life before public ministry ever began. Much of His earthly life was not spent on platforms or in crowds, but in quiet faithfulness and obedience.

That carries something important for people today.

Not every season will feel exciting. Not every assignment will feel significant. Not every act of faithfulness will be seen by others.

But God often does deep work in people during the ordinary hidden seasons of life.

Luke 16:10 (ESV)

“One who is faithful in a very little is also faithful in much…”

Faithfulness in small things matters.

Showing up matters. Integrity matters. Responsibility matters. Serving well matters.

Not because these things earn the love of God, but because over time they help form a life that is stable, trustworthy, disciplined, and rooted more deeply in Him.

And often it is through ordinary daily work that God slowly teaches people how to live faithfully again.
