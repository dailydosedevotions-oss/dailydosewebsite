Daily Dose: Blessed Are

Blessed Are: Those Who Mourn - Part 2

Releases Sunday Aug 30, 2026 at 7:00PM

Matthew 5:4 (ESV)

“Blessed are those who mourn, for they shall be comforted.”

At first, these words can feel hard to understand. Mourning is usually connected with sorrow, loss, grief, and pain. Yet Jesus says those who mourn are blessed. The comfort He promises is not shallow encouragement or temporary emotional relief. It is the comfort that comes when God meets a person honestly in their brokenness.

This Beatitude includes the sorrow of loss, suffering, and human pain because God truly cares about the brokenhearted. But the mourning Jesus describes also reaches deeper. It includes mourning over sin, mourning over distance from God, and mourning over what we become when we live apart from the Lord.

We see this in Peter after he denied Jesus. Peter had boldly declared that he would remain faithful no matter what. But when fear came, he failed openly. His tears were not merely embarrassment; he was grieving that he had denied the One he loved. Yet his story did not end in condemnation. Jesus restored him.

Psalm 51:10-11 (ESV) says, “Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me. Cast me not away from your presence.” David's sorrow led him to repentance and back to the mercy of God.

Sometimes God allows us to feel the weight of what we once ignored: the damage pride causes, the emptiness of sin, and the truth of our dependence on Him. These moments can be painful, but they can also become the beginning of real change.

Jesus promises that those who mourn will be comforted. That comfort may not always mean immediate relief from every circumstance. Often it means the presence of God within the sorrow. He restores, forgives, strengthens, heals, and draws near to those who come to Him honestly.
