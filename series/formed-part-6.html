Daily Dose: FORMED

Part 6 — Charity: Loving Beyond Yourself

Releases Sunday Jul 12, 2026 at 7:00PM

Matthew 25:40 (ESV)

“And the King will answer them, ‘Truly, I say to you, as you did it to one of the least of these my brothers, you did it to me.’”

One of the clearest marks of a life being formed by Christ is not simply knowledge of Scripture, strong opinions, or outward appearance. It is love.

Not shallow love. Not convenient love. Not love only shown when it feels easy.

But real love that moves toward people with compassion, patience, mercy, and humility.

The truth is, without God, the human heart naturally bends inward toward self. People protect themselves. Prioritise themselves. Think about their own comfort, their own needs, their own survival first. And if we are honest, that mindset can still quietly return when Christ is no longer being kept at the centre.

I know in my own life there were years where selfishness completely shaped the way I lived. Addiction only strengthened that mindset. Most decisions revolved around escape, survival, self-preservation, or whatever benefited me in the moment. And honestly, even after coming to Christ, I can still find traces of that same selfishness trying to rise again whenever I stop keeping God at the centre of my life.

That is why charity is not just about giving money.

It is about learning to live beyond yourself.

When Jesus speaks about serving “the least of these,” He reveals something deeply important about the heart of God. Christ consistently moved toward people others ignored, judged, avoided, or looked down on.

Matthew 9:36 (ESV)

“When he saw the crowds, he had compassion for them, because they were harassed and helpless, like sheep without a shepherd.”

Jesus did not only see behaviour. He saw brokenness. He saw pain. He saw people fully.

And the more a person walks closely with Christ, the harder it becomes to remain cold toward people He loves.

That does not mean loving people is always easy.

Sometimes loving people becomes exhausting. Sometimes showing grace feels costly. Sometimes compassion requires patience when frustration would feel easier.

And sometimes pride quietly convinces people they are above the very struggles they once needed mercy for themselves.

That is why charity must always remain connected to humility.

Because people who truly understand the mercy of God toward their own lives begin learning how to extend mercy toward others.

1 Corinthians 13:2 (ESV)

“And if I have prophetic powers, and understand all mysteries and all knowledge, and if I have all faith, so as to remove mountains, but have not love, I am nothing.”

That Scripture is deeply confronting.

A person can know Scripture. Speak well. Serve publicly. Appear spiritually mature.

Yet still become cold, prideful, impatient, and lacking genuine love for people.

But Christ did not call believers simply to appear spiritual.

He called them to love.

Real charity often happens quietly:

listening when someone needs time

serving when nobody notices

helping without needing recognition

remaining patient when tired

treating broken people with dignity

giving grace instead of judgment

These things may appear small to the world, but before God they matter deeply.

And honestly, some of the greatest acts of love people will ever give are not dramatic moments at all. Often they are quiet sacrifices made consistently over time.

Galatians 5:13 (ESV)

“For you were called to freedom, brothers. Only do not use your freedom as an opportunity for the flesh, but through love serve one another.”

The more Christ shapes a person, the more their life slowly begins turning outward instead of inward.

Not perfectly. Not instantly.

But gradually.

Because the love of Christ does not only comfort people.

It transforms the way they begin seeing others.
