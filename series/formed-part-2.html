Daily Dose: FORMED

Part 2 — Prayer: Staying Near to God

Releases Sunday Jun 14, 2026 at 7:00PM

1 Thessalonians 5:17 (ESV)

“Pray without ceasing.”

Prayer is one of the most spoken about parts of the Christian life, yet for many people it becomes one of the hardest things to remain consistent in. Not always because people do not care about God, but because life becomes loud. Distractions grow. Weariness sets in. The mind becomes crowded. And slowly, prayer can begin to feel more like a duty than a relationship.

But when Scripture says:

“Pray without ceasing.”

it is not calling believers into endless religious repetition. It is describing a life that remains aware of God throughout the day. A heart that keeps turning toward Him. A relationship that does not only exist in moments of crisis, but continues in ordinary life.

Prayer is not about impressing God with words. Jesus already warned against that.

Matthew 6:7-8 (ESV)

“And when you pray, do not heap up empty phrases as the Gentiles do, for they think that they will be heard for their many words. Do not be like them, for your Father knows what you need before you ask him.”

God is not looking for performance. He is looking for honesty. There are moments where prayer is full of faith and confidence, and there are moments where prayer is simply, “Lord, help me.” Both matter. Both are heard.

One of the clearest examples of this is found in the life of Daniel. Even in pressure, uncertainty, and opposition, prayer remained part of his life.

Daniel 6:10 (ESV)

“When Daniel knew that the document had been signed, he went to his house where he had windows in his upper chamber open toward Jerusalem. He got down on his knees three times a day and prayed and gave thanks before his God, as he had done previously.”

Daniel’s prayer life was not built only for emergencies. It was built through consistency. Long before the lions’ den ever appeared, prayer had already become part of who he was.

I know in my own life there were seasons where prayer became difficult because so much noise, addiction, distraction, and chaos had filled my mind for years. There were times where sitting still with God felt uncomfortable because I had spent so long trying to escape myself through other things. Sometimes the hardest part was not speaking to God — it was slowing down long enough to actually be honest before Him.

And honestly, there are still days where distractions try to pull my attention everywhere else.

That is where many people struggle today. Prayer often becomes reactive instead of relational. People pray when things fall apart, but distance themselves when life becomes comfortable again. Yet prayer was never meant to be a last resort. It was meant to be ongoing communion with God.

There will be days where prayer feels powerful, and there will be days where it feels dry. Days where words come easily, and days where the heart feels exhausted. But faithfulness in prayer is not built on emotion alone. Sometimes remaining near to God means continuing to come before Him even when feelings fluctuate.

Jesus Himself continually withdrew to pray. Not because He lacked strength, but because communion with the Father was central to His life.

Luke 5:16 (ESV)

“But he would withdraw to desolate places and pray.”

If Jesus made space for prayer, then prayer cannot be treated as something optional in the life of a believer.

Prayer is where dependence grows. It is where pride begins to weaken. It is where burdens are brought honestly before God instead of being silently carried alone. It is where the heart is slowly shaped over time.

Not every prayer will be dramatic. Not every moment will feel emotional. But over time, consistent prayer keeps a person close to God in a world constantly trying to pull attention elsewhere.

Prayer is not about having perfect words.

It is about remaining near to the One who already knows your heart.
