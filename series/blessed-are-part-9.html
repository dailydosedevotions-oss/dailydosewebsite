Daily Dose: Blessed Are

Blessed Are: You When Others Revile You - Part 9

Releases Sunday Oct 18, 2026 at 7:00PM

Matthew 5:11-12 (ESV)

“Blessed are you when others revile you and persecute you and utter all kinds of evil against you falsely on my account.”

Jesus closes the Beatitudes with words that are both deeply comforting and deeply challenging. After speaking about humility, mourning, meekness, righteousness, mercy, purity, peace, and persecution, He now speaks directly to those who will suffer because they belong to Him.

He does not only tell His disciples to endure. He says, “Rejoice and be glad, for your reward is great in heaven.” Heaven sees suffering differently when that suffering comes from faithfulness to Christ.

Jeremiah's life shows this clearly. He preached God's message when many preferred false comfort over repentance. Jeremiah 20:2 (ESV) says, “Then Pashhur beat Jeremiah the prophet, and put him in the stocks.”

The apostles also lived this truth after the resurrection of Christ. Acts 5:41 (ESV) says, “Then they left the presence of the council, rejoicing that they were counted worthy to suffer dishonor for the name.”

Jesus Himself is the perfect example. Though He was without sin, He was rejected, falsely accused, mocked, beaten, abandoned, and crucified. 1 Peter 2:23 (ESV) says, “When he was reviled, he did not revile in return,” and “continued entrusting himself to him who judges justly.”

The Beatitudes begin with surrender and end with endurance. They show how the kingdom of God changes people from the inside out, and Jesus assures His people that no act of faithfulness done for Him is forgotten by the Father.
