Daily Dose: Blessed Are

Blessed Are: Those Persecuted For Righteousness' Sake - Part 8

Releases Sunday Oct 11, 2026 at 7:00PM

Matthew 5:10 (ESV)

“Blessed are those who are persecuted for righteousness' sake, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.”

This is one of the hardest Beatitudes because it speaks about rejection, opposition, pain, and personal cost. Yet Jesus includes persecution in the life of the believer because true righteousness will eventually collide with a world that does not surrender to God.

Jesus is not speaking about suffering caused by arrogance, harshness, foolishness, pride, or needless conflict. He says they are persecuted for righteousness' sake. The blessing is attached to suffering because a person chooses obedience to God over approval from the world.

Stephen's life gives a powerful example. Acts 6:10 (ESV) says, “they could not withstand the wisdom and the Spirit with which he was speaking.” Even as he faced death, Stephen's heart revealed faith and surrender to God.

Acts 7:59-60 (ESV) says, “Lord Jesus, receive my spirit,” and “Lord, do not hold this sin against them.” Even in suffering, his heart reflected the mercy and character of Jesus.

Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego showed the same courage. Daniel 3:17-18 (ESV) says, “Our God whom we serve is able to deliver us,” and “But if not... we will not serve your gods.”

Conflict for its own sake is not the goal. Faithfulness to Christ is the goal. Jesus calls the persecuted blessed because heaven sees differently than the world sees.
