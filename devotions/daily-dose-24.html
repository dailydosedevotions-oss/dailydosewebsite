Daily Dose #24

YES, YOU CAN

May 14, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

PHILIPPIANS 4:13 (ESV)

“I can do all things through him who strengthens me.”

There are moments where doubt feels louder than anything else. You can know what is right, you can want to move forward, but internally something pushes back. It can feel like you are not strong enough, not ready enough, or not capable of stepping into what is in front of you.

PHILIPPIANS 4:13 (ESV) is often repeated, but it carries a depth that is sometimes missed. It is not a statement of self-confidence, it is a statement of dependence. “Through him who strengthens me” changes everything. It shifts the focus away from what you can do on your own and places it on what is possible through Jesus.

That matters, because relying on your own strength will always feel limited. There will always be moments where it is not enough, where pressure builds, or where things feel beyond your reach. But the strength that comes from Jesus is not limited in the same way. It is not based on how you feel in the moment, but on who He is.

Jesus consistently called people beyond what they believed they were capable of, not to expose weakness, but to reveal what was possible through Him.

2 CORINTHIANS 12:9 (ESV) says, “My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.”

That shows that weakness is not the end point. It is often the place where God’s strength becomes most visible. When you recognise your own limitations, it creates space for His strength to be at work in a way that would not happen otherwise.

There will always be reasons to doubt, and there will always be moments where things feel beyond you. But that does not mean the situation is beyond what God can do through you.

The question is not whether you feel able.

It is whether you are willing to rely on the strength that has already been made available.

And when that shift takes place, what once felt out of reach begins to look different, because you are no longer approaching it on your own.
