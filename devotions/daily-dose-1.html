Daily Dose #1

WHAT AM I MISSING?

Apr 21, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

LUKE 19:1–10 (ESV)

“He entered Jericho and was passing through. And behold, there was a man named Zacchaeus. He was a chief tax collector and was rich. And he was seeking to see who Jesus was, but on account of the crowd he could not, because he was small in stature. So he ran on ahead and climbed up into a sycamore tree to see him, for he was about to pass that way. And when Jesus came to the place, he looked up and said to him, ‘Zacchaeus, hurry and come down, for I must stay at your house today.’ So he hurried and came down and received him joyfully. And when they saw it, they all grumbled, ‘He has gone in to be the guest of a man who is a sinner.’ And Zacchaeus stood and said to the Lord, ‘Behold, Lord, the half of my goods I give to the poor. And if I have defrauded anyone of anything, I restore it fourfold.’ And Jesus said to him, ‘Today salvation has come to this house, since he also is a son of Abraham. For the Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost.’”

ECCLESIASTES 3:10 (ESV)

“I have seen the business that God has given to the children of man to be busy with.”

There is a question that can sit quietly underneath a person’s life, and that question is what am I missing. A person can be busy, constantly moving, constantly doing, and still feel like something is not right on the inside. There can be effort, structure, routine, and even progress, yet something deeper remains untouched.

Zacchaeus had position, money, and status, yet what is revealed in LUKE 19:1–10 (ESV) is that even with all of that, he was still seeking. Everything he had built had not satisfied what was going on inside him. There was still something unsettled, something not fulfilled. Instead of ignoring it, he responded to it and positioned himself to see Jesus.

Many people feel that same gap, but instead of seeking Jesus, they try to fill it with other things. Some pursue success, believing achievement will satisfy them. Some rely on relationships, hoping another person will complete what is lacking. Others turn to distraction, addiction, or approval, trying to quiet what they feel inside. The outcome remains the same, because none of those things were designed to fill what only God can.

ECCLESIASTES 3:10 (ESV) shows that life can become full of activity, responsibility, and pressure, but being busy does not mean being fulfilled. A person can fill every hour of the day and still avoid what is really going on underneath. Busyness can cover the reality that something deeper is missing.

When Jesus reached that place, everything changed. He stopped, He looked up, and He called Zacchaeus by name. He did not wait for him to fix himself first. He met him exactly where he was. Zacchaeus responded, and in that moment something shifted inside him that could not be produced by anything he had before.

The feeling of something missing is not something to ignore or suppress. It reveals a deeper need that only Jesus can meet. When that need is filled by Him, everything else begins to fall into its proper place.

Zacchaeus climbed searching, but he came down changed.

That is the difference between a life full of activity and a life that has truly encountered Christ.
