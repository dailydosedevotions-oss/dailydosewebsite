Daily Dose #131

September 2, 2026

MAKERS OF PEACE

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on September 2, 2026.

MATTHEW 5:9 (ESV)

"Blessed are the peacemakers, for they shall be called sons of God."

Jesus blesses the peacemakers. Not the peace pretenders, not the peace avoiders, but the peacemakers. Biblical peace is active, truthful, humble, and rooted in the character of God.

Peacemaking does not mean ignoring sin or pretending wounds do not matter. Peace built on silence alone can leave bitterness growing underneath. The peace of Christ is not fragile dishonesty. It is truth and grace held together.

Being a peacemaker often begins with letting God deal with our own hearts. Pride cannot make peace. Revenge cannot make peace. A tongue that wants to win more than heal will struggle to reflect Jesus.

Christ made peace through the blood of His cross. That means peace is costly. It required sacrifice, humility, and love stronger than hostility. Those who belong to Him are called to carry His ways into their relationships.

Ask God to make you a person who lowers the heat, speaks truth humbly, listens carefully, and seeks reconciliation where possible.

2 Thessalonians 3:16 says, "may the Lord of peace himself give you peace at all times in every way." That means peace is not only something we hope others will create for us. It is something Christ must first rule within us before it can flow through us.

Peacemaking may require courage. Sometimes it means beginning a hard conversation with humility. Sometimes it means refusing to repeat a story that would stir division. Sometimes it means choosing prayer instead of retaliation.

The cross shows us that peace is costly. Jesus did not make peace by pretending sin did not matter. He bore the cost to reconcile sinners to God. When we follow Him, our peacemaking must also be rooted in truth, humility, and sacrificial love.

Ephesians 2:14 says, "he himself is our peace." That means peacemaking is not about controlling everyone else. It begins with surrendering our own pride, tone, motives, and reactions to the One who has made peace with God possible.

There are times when making peace means speaking. There are times when it means staying quiet. There are times when it means apologising, and times when it means refusing to participate in gossip. The peacemaker must walk closely with the Spirit because wisdom is needed.

Peacemaking can feel risky because humility always does. It may require being misunderstood, laying down the need to have the last word, or trusting God with justice. But Jesus says there is blessing in this way because it reflects the family likeness of God.

Peace shaped by Christ is never weakness; it is strength surrendered to love.

Children of God should resemble the Son of God. Blessed are the peacemakers.
