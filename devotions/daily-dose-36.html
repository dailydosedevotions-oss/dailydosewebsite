Daily Dose #36

FAITHFUL IN THE UNKNOWN

May 26, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

HEBREWS 11:12 (ESV)

“Therefore from one man, and him as good as dead, were born descendants as many as the stars of heaven and as many as the innumerable grains of sand by the seashore.”

Faithfulness is not proven when everything makes sense. It is revealed when you choose to trust God even when the situation in front of you looks impossible.

That is what makes HEBREWS 11:12 (ESV) so powerful, because it speaks about Abraham being “as good as dead,” yet God still bringing life, promise, and purpose through him. From a human perspective the situation looked finished. Too late. Too far gone. Yet God was not limited by what man could see.

And that is where faith becomes real.

Because there will be moments where your situation does not look promising. Moments where you cannot see how things will work out, where your strength feels low, and where what God has spoken seems far away from your current reality. But faithfulness is built when you continue trusting Him in the middle of that uncertainty.

Abraham could not produce the promise in his own strength. The entire point was that what God was going to do could only happen through God Himself.

And many times that is exactly where the Lord brings people.

To the end of themselves.

To the place where self-reliance breaks.

To the place where trust has to become deeper than feelings, deeper than logic, and deeper than visible circumstances.

Because if everything always made sense, faith would not be required.

This is why you cannot judge God’s faithfulness by what you currently see. If Abraham had done that, he would have stopped believing long before the promise arrived.

But God remained faithful even when the process was long.

Even when things looked impossible.

Even when human understanding could not fully explain it.

That same truth still applies now.

You may feel like you are in deep water where things are unclear and uncomfortable, but God is not absent there. He is present within it, holding you steady while you continue walking forward.

He is your anchor.

And an anchor is not needed in calm water. It is needed in movement, pressure, uncertainty, and storms. It keeps you grounded when everything around you feels unstable.

That is what God does in your life.

So this becomes the real question: will you trust Him only when things make sense, or will you trust Him even when the situation looks beyond what you can understand?

Because faith is not about having every answer before you move.

It is about knowing the One who has already gone before you.

And when you continue trusting Him through the unknown, you begin to discover that the same God who fulfilled His promise to Abraham is still faithful now, still working in situations that look impossible, and still able to bring life, direction, and purpose where everything once seemed finished.
