Daily Dose #147

September 18, 2026

COMFORT ONE ANOTHER

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on September 18, 2026.

1 THESSALONIANS 4:16-18 (ESV)

"The Lord himself will descend from heaven... Therefore encourage one another with these words."

Paul speaks about the return of Christ and then tells believers to comfort one another with these words. The coming of the Lord is not meant to be only a doctrine we study; it is a hope that strengthens grieving hearts.

Death does not have the final word for those in Christ. The Lord Himself shall descend. The dead in Christ shall rise. We shall be with the Lord. These promises are not fragile. They rest on the risen Saviour.

This comfort does not remove every tear today, but it gives every tear a horizon. Grief is real, but so is resurrection. Waiting is hard, but so is the promise. The separation is painful, but it is not eternal for those who belong to Jesus.

Encourage someone with hope that is bigger than this life. Speak gently. Sit with those who mourn. Do not offer shallow words, but do offer the solid promise of Christ.

The Lord Himself is coming. His people will be gathered to Him. And so shall we ever be with the Lord.

Paul wrote in 1 Thessalonians 4:13 to believers who were grieving, "that you may not grieve as others do who have no hope." He did not tell them not to grieve at all. He told them not to grieve as others do who have no hope. Christian hope does not erase sorrow, but it changes its horizon.

The phrase "the Lord himself" is deeply comforting. He does not send a distant message only; He comes. The same Jesus who died and rose again will gather His people to Himself. Resurrection is not an idea floating in the air. It is anchored in Him.

This is why we comfort one another with these words. Not with shallow phrases, not with denial, but with the solid promise that death will not have the final word over those who are in Christ.

This hope is especially tender for those carrying grief. The gospel does not ask us to pretend loss does not hurt. Psalm 56:8 says, "You have kept count of my tossings; put my tears in your bottle." Tears are not the enemy of faith.

But resurrection means grief does not get the last word. The grave is not stronger than Christ. The trumpet will sound, the dead in Christ will rise, and the people of God will be with the Lord forever. That is not shallow comfort. That is solid ground.

Encouragement like this should be handled gently. People who are grieving do not need rushed answers. They need presence, compassion, and the steady hope of Scripture offered with tenderness. The promise is strong enough to be spoken softly.

Comfort one another with these words.
