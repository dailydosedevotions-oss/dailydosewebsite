Daily Dose #93

July 26, 2026

SERVING GOD WHOLEHEARTEDLY

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on July 26, 2026.

COLOSSIANS 3:23–24 (ESV)

“Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men, knowing that from the Lord you will receive the inheritance as your reward. You are serving the Lord Christ.”

Serving God is not an emotion that comes and goes with our circumstances. There will be days when we feel motivated and days when we feel tired. There will be seasons when serving Christ feels exciting and others when it feels unnoticed or even difficult. Yet our commitment to Him was never meant to depend upon how we feel. It is rooted in who He is and in what He has done for us.

One of the greatest dangers in the Christian life is becoming discouraged when our efforts seem unnoticed. We can begin comparing ourselves to others, wondering whether our faithfulness really matters. Yet Scripture reminds us that our service is never ultimately for people. Every act of obedience, every unseen sacrifice, every quiet moment of faithfulness is first and foremost an offering to the Lord.

There is another danger that often follows discouragement, and that is becoming comfortable. It is possible to settle into a place where we stop growing spiritually because we become satisfied with where we are. God has not called His people simply to stand still. He calls us to continue growing, continue serving, and continue becoming more like Christ. There is always another step of obedience to take and another opportunity to trust Him more deeply.

God does not bless us simply so that we can keep His blessings to ourselves. He blesses His people so that they might become a blessing to others. Every gift He gives, every opportunity He opens, and every lesson He teaches is intended to bring glory to Him and encouragement to those around us. A life surrendered to Christ becomes a life that reflects His generosity, His love, and His grace.

This is why Paul encourages believers to do everything wholeheartedly, “as for the Lord and not for men.” When Christ remains our focus, our service is no longer controlled by recognition, applause, or circumstances. We serve because we love Him. We persevere because He has been faithful to us. We continue because we know that nothing done for His glory is ever wasted.

As we keep our eyes fixed on Jesus, our perspective begins to change. We stop measuring success by what others see and begin measuring faithfulness by our obedience to Him. The more we seek to honour Christ in the ordinary moments of life, the more we discover that He is shaping us through them.

Do not allow discouragement to steal your passion.

Do not allow comfort to stop your growth.

Keep serving Christ with your whole heart, knowing that the One you serve sees every act of faithfulness and promises that your labour in Him is never in vain.
