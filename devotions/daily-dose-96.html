Daily Dose #96

July 29, 2026

GRACE FOR THE HUMBLE

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on July 29, 2026.

JAMES 4:6 (ESV)

“But he gives more grace. Therefore it says, ‘God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble.’”

The Christian life is not something we drift through. Our relationship with Christ grows as we intentionally seek Him, spend time in His Word, and humble ourselves before Him. Just as a healthy relationship requires time, attention, and commitment, so does our walk with God. We cannot expect to experience spiritual growth while neglecting the very things that help us grow.

James reminds us that “God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble.” Humility is more than simply thinking less of ourselves. It is recognising our need for God every single day. A humble heart understands that it cannot grow apart from Christ and willingly submits to His leading. God delights in giving grace to those who acknowledge their dependence upon Him.

Seeking God should never depend upon how we feel. There will be days when reading Scripture feels easy and days when it feels difficult. There will be seasons when prayer comes naturally and seasons when it requires perseverance. Yet our commitment to Christ is not built upon our emotions but upon the truth that He is worthy of our devotion. As we continue seeking Him with all our hearts, He shapes our lives, renews our thinking, and gives us the wisdom we need to keep moving forward in our walk with Him.

This is especially true when we face difficult seasons or recovery from past mistakes. God has not called us simply to survive; He has called us to grow. His Word provides the direction, wisdom, correction, and encouragement we need to become more like Christ. The more we invest in our relationship with Him, the more we begin seeing His work within us.

One of the greatest dangers in the Christian life is becoming distracted. Our attention can easily be drawn towards worries, ambitions, opinions, or the endless demands of life until Christ is no longer our primary focus. Yet every distraction that pulls our eyes away from Jesus also pulls our hearts away from the peace, joy, and purpose that are found in Him. The enemy is often content to distract us if he cannot defeat us.

Keep your eyes fixed on Christ. Do not lose sight of the work He is doing in your life or the purpose He has placed before you. The closer you walk with Him, the more clearly you will see His faithfulness, His grace, and His love. As your relationship with Him deepens, you will discover that the life He is calling you to is far greater than anything this world could ever offer.

The truth is simple.

What you put into your walk with Christ is what you will get out of it.

Seek Him wholeheartedly.

Keep your eyes on Him.

And watch how He transforms your life by His grace.
