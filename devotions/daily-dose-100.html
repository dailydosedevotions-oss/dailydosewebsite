WHAT IS FORGIVENESS?

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 2, 2026.

EPHESIANS 4:31–32 (ESV)

“Let all bitterness and wrath and anger and clamor and slander be put away from you, along with all malice. Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you.”

Forgiveness is one of the most misunderstood words in the Christian life. Many people believe forgiveness is simply a feeling that comes after the hurt has disappeared. Others think it means pretending the offence never happened or acting as though the pain was not real. The Bible teaches something very different. Forgiveness is not primarily a feeling. It is an act of the will. It is the conscious decision to release the offence into God's hands rather than allowing it to continue ruling our hearts.

This does not mean that forgiveness is easy. Some wounds run deep, and some words or actions leave lasting scars. God never asks us to pretend that those things did not happen. Instead, He calls us to trust Him enough to surrender our right to hold on to bitterness, believing that He is the righteous Judge who sees all things and will deal with every wrong in His perfect way and in His perfect time.

Without forgiveness, hurts and regrets begin to accumulate within us. What may have started as one painful experience slowly grows into resentment, anger, and bitterness that affects every area of our lives. We carry those burdens into our relationships, our homes, our workplaces, and even into our walk with God. The person who wounded us may have moved on long ago, yet we continue carrying the weight every single day.

Paul understood this when he wrote, “Let all bitterness and wrath and anger... be put away from you.” Those attitudes do not simply disappear on their own. They must be surrendered to God. Forgiveness is the decision to stop allowing yesterday's wounds to control today's life. It is choosing obedience even when our emotions are still catching up.

The greatest example of forgiveness is found in Jesus Christ. While hanging on the cross, He bore the punishment for our sins so that we could be forgiven and reconciled to God. We did nothing to earn that forgiveness. It was given freely because of His grace. When we remember how much we have been forgiven, it changes the way we view those who have wronged us.

Forgiveness is not denying the hurt.

It is refusing to let the hurt become your master.

When we choose to forgive, we are trusting God with what we cannot change and placing our confidence in His justice rather than our own. It may not remove the memory overnight, but it begins breaking the chains that bitterness tries to wrap around our hearts.

Forgiveness is not weakness.

It is an act of faith.

It is choosing God's way over our feelings, believing that His grace is powerful enough to heal what we cannot heal ourselves.

Tomorrow, we will look at what forgiveness produces and discover that while forgiveness is first an act of obedience, it also becomes the pathway to true freedom.
