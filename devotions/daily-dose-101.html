THE FREEDOM OF FORGIVENESS

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 3, 2026.

COLOSSIANS 3:13 (ESV)

“Bearing with one another and, if one has a complaint against another, forgiving each other; as the Lord has forgiven you, so you also must forgive.”

Yesterday we saw that forgiveness is not a feeling but an act of the will. Today we discover what forgiveness produces. Many people view forgiveness as something that benefits the person who caused the hurt, but the Bible shows us that forgiveness also brings freedom to the one who chooses to forgive.

One of the greatest misunderstandings about forgiveness is believing that it means letting someone "off the hook." Forgiveness does not mean pretending the offence never happened, excusing sinful behaviour, or denying the pain that was caused. Nor does it always mean that trust is immediately restored. Trust is rebuilt over time, but forgiveness is a decision that can be made the moment we place the situation into God's hands.

When we refuse to forgive, bitterness quietly begins to take root. It occupies our thoughts, shapes our attitudes, steals our peace, and slowly affects every area of our lives. We may believe we are holding on to the other person's offence, but in reality, the offence is holding on to us. Unforgiveness keeps old wounds alive long after the event has passed.

Forgiveness changes that. It is the decision to release the burden we were never meant to carry. It is choosing not to allow someone else's actions to continue controlling our hearts. The person who wronged us may never apologise or even acknowledge what they have done, but our freedom is not dependent upon their response. It is found in our obedience to Christ.

Paul reminds believers, “As the Lord has forgiven you, so you also must forgive.” We forgive because we have first been forgiven. Every one of us has received mercy that we could never earn. Christ did not wait until we deserved His grace before going to the cross. He willingly gave Himself so that our sins could be forgiven and our relationship with God restored. His forgiveness becomes both our example and our motivation.

Choosing to forgive is rarely easy, but obedience to God often requires us to take steps before our emotions catch up. As we surrender our hurt to Him, He begins doing the work of healing that only He can accomplish. The bitterness that once weighed us down gradually gives way to peace, and the wounds we thought would never heal become places where God's grace is displayed.

Forgiveness is not about changing the past.

It is about refusing to let the past control your future.

When you forgive, you are not saying that what happened was acceptable. You are saying that you trust God more than your pain. You are placing the situation into the hands of the One who judges perfectly and loves completely.

Forgiveness is not weakness.

It is freedom.

It is one of the clearest demonstrations of the grace that Christ has shown to every one of us, and as we extend that grace to others, we discover that the heart set free is often our own.
