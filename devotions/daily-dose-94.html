Daily Dose #94

July 27, 2026

GUARD YOUR HEART

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on July 27, 2026.

PROVERBS 4:23 (ESV)

“Keep your heart with all vigilance, for from it flow the springs of life.”

The Bible places great importance on the condition of the heart. While people often focus on outward appearance, achievements, or reputation, God looks far deeper. Scripture teaches that our words, attitudes, decisions, and actions all flow from what is happening within our hearts. This is why Solomon gives this wise instruction: “Keep your heart with all vigilance, for from it flow the springs of life.”

To guard your heart does not mean isolating yourself from the world or becoming suspicious of everyone around you. It means carefully protecting what shapes your thoughts, your desires, and your affections. Every day we are influenced by the conversations we have, the things we watch, the advice we listen to, and the people we spend time with. Over time, these influences help shape the direction of our lives, whether for good or for harm.

This is why PROVERBS 19:20 (ESV) says, “Listen to advice and accept instruction, that you may gain wisdom in the future.” A wise person is not someone who believes they know everything. A wise person remains teachable. They recognise that God often uses His Word and mature believers to bring correction, encouragement, and direction.

At the same time, Scripture also warns us to exercise discernment. PROVERBS 12:15 (ESV) reminds us, “The way of a fool is right in his own eyes, but a wise man listens to advice.” There is a difference between following every opinion we hear and seeking godly wisdom. The heart that is guarded is a heart that measures everything against the truth of God's Word.

The book of Psalms opens by describing the life of the person who chooses this path. PSALM 1:1–3 (ESV) says, “Blessed is the man who walks not in the counsel of the wicked... but his delight is in the law of the LORD, and on his law he meditates day and night. He is like a tree planted by streams of water that yields its fruit in its season.” A healthy tree produces healthy fruit because its roots are drawing from a healthy source. In the same way, a heart rooted in God's Word will gradually produce a life that reflects His character.

Guarding your heart is not about living in fear. It is about living intentionally. It means asking yourself what is shaping your thinking, what is influencing your decisions, and whether your heart is becoming more like Christ or more like the world around you. Every choice either strengthens your walk with God or slowly pulls your attention elsewhere.

No one drifts into spiritual maturity. Growth requires a heart that remains open to God's instruction, willing to receive correction, and eager to follow His ways. As we continue walking with Christ, the Holy Spirit works within us, shaping our desires so that what flows from our hearts increasingly reflects the life of Jesus.

The heart is the wellspring of our lives.

Guard it carefully.

Fill it with God's truth.

And allow Him to shape it into a heart that honours Him in every area of life.
