August 7, 2026

THE MAGNITUDE OF GOD'S LOVE

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 7, 2026.

JOHN 3:16 (ESV)

“For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life.”

There are few verses in Scripture as well known as John 3:16, yet its depth can never be exhausted. In one verse we are given a glimpse of the greatness of God's love—a love so vast that it reaches into every part of our lives and stretches beyond time itself. The more we look at this verse, the more we begin to appreciate the magnitude of the love God has shown towards us in Jesus Christ.

God's love is first seen in its action. John does not simply tell us that God loves the world; he tells us that God gave. True love is never merely spoken about. It acts. It sacrifices. It gives. The greatest demonstration of God's love was not found in words alone but in the giving of His only Son to bear our sin upon the cross. Every act of grace we receive flows from that one perfect act of sacrificial love.

God's love is also seen in its reach. John writes that God so loved the world. His invitation is not limited to one nation, one background, or one kind of person. The Gospel reaches the broken, the forgotten, the religious, the rebellious, the rich, and the poor alike. No life is beyond the reach of His grace, and no person is too far away to be welcomed by Christ when they come to Him in faith.

Finally, God's love is eternal in its scope. Jesus did not come merely to improve our present circumstances or make life a little easier. He came so that “whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life.” God's love reaches beyond this present life and offers something that nothing else can provide: forgiveness of sins, reconciliation with God, and the promise of everlasting life in His presence.

The cross reminds us that God's love is not measured by our worthiness but by His grace. We did not earn His love, and we could never deserve it. Yet while we were still sinners, God acted in mercy by sending His Son to save us. Every believer stands as a testimony to the greatness of that love.

The more we understand God's love, the more it changes the way we live. It teaches us to trust Him more deeply, worship Him more sincerely, and love others more faithfully because we have first been loved by Him.

God's love acted.

God's love reached.

God's love secured eternity.

Stand in awe of that love today, and never lose sight of the incredible grace that has been poured out through Jesus Christ.
