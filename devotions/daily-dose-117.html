Daily Dose #117

August 19, 2026

TRUE GAIN

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 19, 2026.

1 TIMOTHY 6:6 (ESV)

"But godliness with contentment is great gain."

The world often teaches us that gain is measured by having more: more comfort, more money, more recognition, more control. Scripture speaks differently. Godliness with contentment is great gain.

That kind of gain cannot be stolen by a difficult season. It is not dependent on applause, possessions, or comparison. It grows in the heart that belongs to God and learns to rest in His care.

Contentment does not make us lazy, and godliness does not make us joyless. Together they form a life that is free. Free from needing everything to go our way. Free from chasing what cannot satisfy. Free to enjoy God's gifts without worshipping them.

Paul warns that the love of money pierces the soul with many sorrows. The issue is not only what we have, but what has our heart. If our peace depends on always having more, we will never be at rest.

Ask the Lord to show you what you have been calling gain. Let Him purify your desires and teach you the beauty of a life that values Him above all else.

Paul continues in 1 Timothy 6:9-10 by warning that "those who desire to be rich fall into temptation," and that "the love of money is a root of all kinds of evils." The issue is not only money; it is what the heart believes money can give: safety, identity, power, comfort, or worth.

Jesus said in Matthew 6:24, "You cannot serve God and money." That is a strong word, but it is a merciful one. He knows that whatever becomes our master will shape our peace. When Christ is our treasure, money can become a tool rather than a throne.

Great gain, then, is not measured by how much we can gather around ourselves. It is measured by a heart that belongs to God, walks in godliness, and rests in His provision. That kind of gain cannot be taken by changing circumstances.

This is a needed challenge in a restless world. We are constantly invited to believe that the next thing will finally settle us: the next purchase, next season, next opportunity, next level. But if the heart is not learning contentment in Christ, the next thing will eventually become another place of thirst.

Godliness with contentment is great gain because it gives the soul a centre that cannot be moved by comparison. The heart can celebrate another person without feeling robbed. It can receive little with gratitude and much with humility.

This kind of contentment becomes a witness. In a world driven by craving, a believer who can receive with gratitude, release with trust, and rejoice without comparison quietly testifies that Christ is real treasure.

True gain is not having everything. It is having Christ and a heart content in Him.
