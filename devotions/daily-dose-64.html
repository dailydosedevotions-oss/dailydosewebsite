Daily Dose #64

June 27, 2026

I'M NOT WHERE I USED TO BE

1 PETER 2:11–17 (ESV)

“Beloved, I urge you as sojourners and exiles to abstain from the passions of the flesh, which wage war against your soul. Keep your conduct among the Gentiles honorable, so that when they speak against you as evildoers, they may see your good deeds and glorify God on the day of visitation.”

One of the easiest traps to fall into as believers is becoming discouraged by where we are not instead of recognising what God has already done. We can become so focused on the areas where we still need to grow that we fail to see the evidence of God's grace already at work in our lives. Peter reminds believers that they are now living as “sojourners and exiles.” Their identity has changed. Their citizenship is no longer rooted in the values of the world around them but in the kingdom of God.

This change does not happen without a struggle. Peter says believers are to “abstain from the passions of the flesh, which wage war against your soul.” The Christian life involves a real battle between the old way of living and the new life Christ is producing within us. Old habits, old attitudes, old desires, and old ways of thinking do not disappear overnight. Yet the presence of that battle is often evidence that God is actively working within us. Before Christ, many people simply followed whatever felt right in the moment. Now there is conviction where there was once indifference, a desire to obey where there was once resistance, and a growing awareness that God is calling us to something greater.

Peter then points believers toward the outward evidence of this transformation. He says, “Keep your conduct among the Gentiles honorable.” The work God does within us is meant to become visible through the way we live. Our words, attitudes, choices, and actions all become opportunities to reflect the character of Christ. People may not immediately listen to what we believe, but they will often notice how we live. They will see integrity when compromise would be easier, forgiveness when bitterness would seem justified, and humility where pride would once have ruled.

This is why Peter continues in 1 Peter 2:15, saying, “For this is the will of God, that by doing good you should put to silence the ignorance of foolish people.” God's will is not only that believers know His truth but that they live in a way that displays His truth. A transformed life becomes a testimony to the transforming power of Christ.

Growth is rarely instant. God often works patiently and deeply, shaping character over time. He uses seasons of challenge, correction, waiting, and perseverance to form His people into the likeness of Christ. There are moments when progress feels slow and seasons when we wonder if anything is changing at all. Yet when we stop and look back, we often realise that God has been doing far more than we could see at the time.

The things that once controlled us do not have the same grip they once had. The things that once defined us no longer carry the same authority. The priorities that once dominated our lives begin giving way to a greater desire to follow Christ and honour Him.

The Christian life is not about comparing your journey to someone else's. It is about faithfully following Jesus one step at a time and trusting Him to complete the work He has begun. As you continue walking with Him, obeying His Word, and surrendering to His leading, transformation takes place.

You may not be where you want to be, but by the grace of God, you are not where you used to be.
