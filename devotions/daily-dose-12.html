Daily Dose #12

Let God Search Your Heart

May 2, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

PSALM 139:23–24 (ESV)

“Search me, O God, and know my heart! Try me and know my thoughts! And see if there be any grievous way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting!”

There is a level of honesty that goes deeper than what is said out loud. It is the kind of honesty that happens within, where thoughts, motives, and patterns are actually faced rather than avoided.

It is easy to stay on the surface, to keep things moving, to avoid looking too closely at what is really going on inside. Distraction can cover a lot. Routine can hide a lot. But what is left unaddressed does not disappear, it continues to shape direction quietly.

PSALM 139:23–24 (ESV) is a different approach. It is not self-protection, it is invitation. Asking God to search the heart is not about exposure for the sake of it, it is about being led into something better.

That kind of honesty can feel uncomfortable at first. It means letting go of control over the narrative, and allowing truth to speak clearly. But without it, growth becomes limited. Change stays surface-level.

Jesus always dealt with what was real, not just what was visible.

JOHN 8:32 (ESV) says, “and you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free.”

Freedom does not come from avoiding truth. It comes from facing it and allowing it to do its work. That includes the areas that are easy to ignore, the patterns that have become normal, and the thoughts that have gone unchecked.

Being honest with yourself is not about tearing yourself down.

It is about opening the door for God to build something stronger.

And when that happens, what once felt hidden no longer has the same hold.

Because truth does not trap you.

It frees you to move forward differently.
