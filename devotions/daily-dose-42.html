Daily Dose #42

STAY ON THE NARROW PATH (PART 2)

Jun 5, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

HEBREWS 11:1–6 (ESV)

“Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen. For by it the people of old received their commendation. By faith we understand that the universe was created by the word of God, so that what is seen was not made out of things that are visible.

By faith Abel offered to God a more acceptable sacrifice than Cain, through which he was commended as righteous, God commending him by accepting his gifts. And through his faith, though he died, he still speaks. By faith Enoch was taken up so that he should not see death, and he was not found, because God had taken him. Now before he was taken he was commended as having pleased God.

And without faith it is impossible to please Him, for whoever would draw near to God must believe that He exists and that He rewards those who seek Him.”

Faith and trust are deeply connected. You cannot truly trust God without faith, and real faith will always lead you into deeper trust. One of the hardest parts of following God is learning to trust Him before we fully understand what He is doing. We naturally want explanations, reassurance, visible proof, and certainty before we surrender fully, but faith often requires us to continue walking even when we cannot yet see the outcome clearly.

There are moments in life where we pray, believe, and wait, but when answers do not come immediately, fear begins to speak louder than faith. Questions start filling our minds. We wonder if God is listening, whether He still sees us, or whether we should start taking a different path altogether. Yet real trust is formed when we continue walking with God in seasons where clarity has not yet arrived.

That is why HEBREWS 11:1 (ESV) says, “faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen.” Faith keeps walking even when emotions are unstable and circumstances are unclear. Faith does not mean you understand everything, it means you continue trusting the One who does.

Many people lose heart because the narrow path feels difficult. The world offers easier roads and quicker comfort. Old lifestyles, old habits, addictions, unhealthy relationships, pride, anger, compromise, and familiar coping mechanisms can all begin to look appealing again when the process of change becomes uncomfortable. The broad road tells us to follow feelings, avoid discomfort, and return to whatever satisfies us in the moment, but the narrow path calls us into surrender, obedience, healing, and transformation.

There are people reading this who understand exactly what it feels like to stand between those two roads. One road constantly tries to pull you back toward the old version of yourself, while the other calls you forward into the life God is building within you. Following Jesus does not always feel easy in the beginning because growth often requires letting go of the very things we once leaned on for comfort.

Sometimes following God means being misunderstood by people around you.

Sometimes it means walking away from environments that once felt normal.

Sometimes it means trusting God when you cannot yet see where the road is leading.

But this is where faith becomes more than words.

Because “without faith it is impossible to please Him” and “whoever would draw near to God must believe that He exists and that He rewards those who seek Him.” The narrow path is walked by faith, not by feelings.

And while the broad road may seem easier for a moment, it cannot give what the narrow path leads to.

Life.

Real freedom.

Real transformation.

Real relationship with Jesus.

And when you continue walking with Him, even through uncertainty, even through struggle, and even through seasons where clarity feels distant, you begin to discover that the narrow path is not empty ground. Christ Himself walks with you on it, strengthening you, leading you, and shaping you into the person God has called you to become
