Daily Dose #53

THE INVITATION STILL STANDS

Jun 16, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

LUKE 14:16–23 (ESV)

“But he said to him, ‘A man once gave a great banquet and invited many… And the master said to the servant, “Go out to the highways and hedges and compel people to come in, that my house may be filled.”’”

Jesus tells the parable of a great banquet to reveal the heart of God toward people. A table was prepared. An invitation was given. A place was made ready. Yet many who were invited became distracted by other things and slowly treated the invitation as unimportant.

That still happens today.

People become consumed with work, money, comfort, relationships, success, pride, distractions, addiction, fear, or the pressure of everyday life while continuing to push God further into the background. Some assume there will always be another time to seek Him seriously later. Others believe they have wandered too far from Him already. Yet the invitation of God continues going out.

In LUKE 14:18 (ESV), people begin making excuses for why they cannot come. One focuses on possessions. Another focuses on responsibilities. Another focuses on personal priorities. None of those things were necessarily evil on their own, but they became more important than responding to the invitation being given.

That is the danger of distraction spiritually. Not everything that pulls people away from God looks obviously destructive at first. Sometimes people slowly drift simply because other things keep taking priority over intimacy with Him.

Yet what stands out powerfully in this parable is the response of the master. He continues inviting people. He continues making room. He continues pursuing those who were overlooked, broken, rejected, forgotten, and distant.

That reveals the heart of God.

There are people who believe they are too far gone for God to still want them. Some carry shame from their past. Others feel spiritually numb after years of compromise, addiction, bitterness, disappointment, or running from God. Some people quietly believe they missed their chance a long time ago.

But the invitation still stands.

Jesus did not go to the cross only for people who appear outwardly clean, strong, or religious. He came for the lost, the broken, the weary, the sinner, and the person who knows they need mercy. The Gospel has always been an invitation into relationship with God through Christ, and God is not reluctantly calling people toward Himself. He desires restoration, forgiveness, healing, and relationship with those who genuinely turn toward Him.

Many people keep delaying surrender because they believe they need to fix themselves first before coming to God. Yet Scripture continually shows the opposite. People do not clean themselves before coming to Jesus. They come to Jesus so He can begin changing them.

The invitation still stands.

Grace is still available.

Mercy is still available.

And there is still room at the table.
