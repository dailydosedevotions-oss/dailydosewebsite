Daily Dose #43

WHAT REMAINS WHEN EVERYTHING IS SHAKEN

Jun 6, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

HEBREWS 12:26–27 (ESV)

“At that time his voice shook the earth, but now he has promised, ‘Yet once more I will shake not only the earth but also the heavens.’ This phrase, ‘Yet once more,’ indicates the removal of things that are shaken—that is, things that have been made—in order that the things that cannot be shaken may remain.”

There are seasons where everything begins to move. What felt stable starts to shift, what felt secure begins to fall, and what you relied on no longer holds the same weight. But the shaking is not without purpose, because HEBREWS 12:27 (ESV) — “the removal of things that are shaken… in order that the things that cannot be shaken may remain” shows that what is happening is not random, it is revealing, God is allowing what is temporary to be removed so that what is real can be seen clearly.

And this is where your response matters, because when things begin to shake, you will either run back to what is familiar, or you will move toward what is true. You cannot just run from something, you have to run toward something, because if you only try to remove what is wrong without replacing it with what is right, you will always feel the pull to go back. That is why this is not just about what is being taken away, it is about what you are stepping into.

This is where faith becomes real, because HEBREWS 11:6 (ESV) — “without faith it is impossible to please him” shows that you will not always understand what God is doing, you will not always see the full picture, but you choose to trust Him within it. And that trust is what keeps you steady, because JAMES 1:2–4 (ESV) — “the testing of your faith produces steadfastness” shows that what you are walking through is not there to break you, it is there to build you, forming something in you that would not be produced any other way.

This brings it to one clear place, because the shaking is not the end of the story, it is the process that reveals the foundation, and when everything that can be shaken is removed, what remains will show what your life is actually built on. If it is built on what is temporary, it will fall with it, but if it is built on God, it will stand, not because the situation became easy, but because your foundation never changed. And when you choose, in the middle of uncertainty, to trust Him, to walk by faith, and to step into what He is doing instead of retreating to what is familiar, you will not be left unstable or lost, because the same God who allows the shaking is the one who establishes what remains, and what He builds in you through it will not move, will not collapse, and will not fail, because it is rooted in Him and not in what was never meant to last.
