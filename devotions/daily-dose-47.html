Daily Dose #47

LET THE SHEPHERD FIND YOU

Jun 10, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

LUKE 15:4-7 (ESV)

“What man of you, having a hundred sheep, if he has lost one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the open country, and go after the one that is lost, until he finds it? And when he has found it, he lays it on his shoulders, rejoicing. And when he comes home, he calls together his friends and his neighbors, saying to them, ‘Rejoice with me, for I have found my sheep that was lost.’ Just so, I tell you, there will be more joy in heaven over one sinner who repents than over ninety-nine righteous persons who need no repentance.”

One of the enemy’s greatest lies is convincing people that once they drift far enough from God, they are no longer worth pursuing. Many people quietly carry the belief that they have failed too much, wandered too far, or become too spiritually cold for God to still want them. Yet Jesus tells the parable of the lost sheep to reveal the exact opposite.

In LUKE 15:4 Jesus says the shepherd leaves the ninety-nine and “go[es] after the one that is lost, until he finds it.” That reveals the heart of God toward people who have wandered. God does not become indifferent toward the lost. He pursues them. He searches for them. He continues reaching toward them even when they have drifted far from where they once were spiritually.

There are people who once felt close to God but now feel distant from Him. Life became heavy. Disappointment entered in. Sin slowly crept back in. Distractions grew louder than prayer. Pain hardened the heart. The presence of God that once felt near now feels distant, and many begin trying to fix themselves in their own strength rather than honestly returning to Jesus.

The danger is that people often become more focused on the problem than the One who can heal the problem. Fear, shame, guilt, addiction, anxiety, bitterness, and disappointment can consume the mind until a person slowly drifts further away from the peace, love, and joy found in the presence of God. Yet the Shepherd still comes looking.

Jesus says in LUKE 15:5, “And when he has found it, he lays it on his shoulders, rejoicing.” The shepherd does not respond with rejection when he finds the lost sheep. He responds with joy. That matters deeply because many people expect condemnation from God when they have fallen, drifted, or struggled. Yet Christ continually reveals mercy toward those who return to Him.

Sometimes people become spiritually exhausted because they are trying to force themselves back into peace through human effort alone. They chase old feelings, old moments, or old experiences while remaining restless internally. However, peace is not rediscovered through striving harder in human strength. Peace is found by returning to the Shepherd.

Many believers drift slowly rather than suddenly. A person stops praying consistently, stops reading Scripture deeply, stops being honest before God, and slowly begins allowing spiritual numbness to replace intimacy with Christ. Yet even then, Jesus continues calling people back toward Himself.

There are also people who have built impossible standards for themselves spiritually. They become frustrated by weakness, failure, inconsistency, or ongoing battles, forgetting that growth with God is often a process. Instead of resting in the grace of Christ, they become trapped trying to earn peace through performance. But the Gospel has never been about saving ourselves through perfection. It is about surrendering ourselves to the One who already came to save us.

LUKE 15:7 says, “there will be more joy in heaven over one sinner who repents.” Heaven rejoices when people return to God. Restoration is not something God reluctantly allows. It is something His heart welcomes because Jesus came to seek and save the lost.

There are people reading this who feel spiritually tired from constantly running, striving, resisting, or trying to carry life alone. Yet Jesus never asked people to save themselves. He asked them to come to Him and allow the Shepherd to lead them home again.

The lost sheep did not restore itself.

The shepherd found it.

And the same Shepherd is still pursuing people today with mercy, patience, grace, and love. So stop running from the presence of God, stop believing the lie that you are too far gone, and stop trying to carry burdens alone that were never meant to be carried without Christ. Open your heart before Him again and allow the Shepherd to bring you back to the place where peace, restoration, joy, and true life are found in His presence.
