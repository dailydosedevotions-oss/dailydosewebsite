Daily Dose #28

GOD IS STILL FAITHFUL

May 18, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

TIMOTHY 2:13 (ESV)

“If we are faithless, he remains faithful— for he cannot deny himself.”

There are times where your own consistency can feel uncertain. You may start strong, but over time things can shift. Focus can drift, discipline can weaken, and there can be moments where you question where you stand or how steady you really are.

2 TIMOTHY 2:13 (ESV) speaks into that honestly. It does not ignore human weakness, it acknowledges it. There are moments where faith feels strong, and there are moments where it feels stretched or uncertain. But the faithfulness of God is not dependent on those fluctuations.

That matters, because relying on your own consistency alone will always feel unstable. There will be good days and difficult days, clear moments and uncertain ones. But God’s faithfulness does not move with those changes. It remains steady, not because of what you do, but because of who He is.

Jesus showed that same faithfulness in how He dealt with people. He did not walk away when they struggled, doubted, or fell short. He remained present, continued to call them forward, and continued to work with them even when they were not at their best.

LAMENTATIONS 3:22–23 (ESV) says, “The steadfast love of the Lord never ceases; his mercies never come to an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness.”

That shows that God’s faithfulness is not something that runs out or becomes limited. It is consistent, renewed, and available each day. That means every new day is not based on how the previous one went, but on the fact that His faithfulness remains the same.

There can be a tendency to focus on what has not been done well, or where things have not been consistent. But staying there can pull attention away from what is actually holding everything in place.

God’s faithfulness is not fragile.

It does not depend on perfect performance.

It remains steady, even when things feel uncertain, and that steadiness is what allows you to keep moving forward with confidence in something greater than yourself.
