Daily Dose #29

DON’T GO BACK

May 19, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

LUKE 9:62 (ESV)

“Jesus said to him, ‘No one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God.’”

There are moments where moving forward feels right, but looking back still carries a pull. The past can feel familiar, even when it was not leading anywhere good. It can hold memories, habits, or ways of thinking that are difficult to fully let go of, especially when the present feels uncertain.

LUKE 9:62 (ESV) brings a clear direction. Moving forward requires focus. Looking back does not just affect what you remember, it affects how you move. When attention is divided, direction becomes unsteady.

That does not mean the past is ignored or forgotten completely. It means it is no longer the place you are taking direction from. What has already been left behind cannot lead you forward, even if it feels familiar or comfortable.

Jesus consistently called people into something new, not to leave them stuck between two directions, but to move them fully into what was ahead.

PHILIPPIANS 3:13–14 (ESV) says, “But one thing I do: forgetting what lies behind and straining forward to what lies ahead, I press on toward the goal…”

That shows that forward movement requires intention. It is not automatic. There is a decision to release what has been and to commit to what is ahead, even when the full picture is not yet clear.

There can be a temptation to revisit what has already been left, to reconsider it, or to keep it close “just in case.” But that keeps something open that is meant to be closed. It divides attention and makes it harder to move with clarity.

Moving forward does not mean everything feels easy or certain.

It means the direction has been decided.

And once that direction is set, staying focused on what is ahead allows you to continue building into something new, rather than being pulled back into what no longer belongs in your life.
