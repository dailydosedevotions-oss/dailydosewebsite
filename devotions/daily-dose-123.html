Daily Dose #123

August 25, 2026

WALK UPRIGHTLY

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 25, 2026.

PROVERBS 10:9 (ESV)

"Whoever walks in integrity walks securely, but he who makes his ways crooked will be found out."

Integrity brings a steadiness that dishonesty can never provide. This proverb teaches that the one who walks uprightly walks securely. There is safety in a life that does not have to hide, twist, cover, or pretend.

Walking uprightly does not mean living perfectly. It means living honestly before God. When you sin, you confess it. When you are wrong, you own it. When temptation comes, you do not make peace with darkness.

The crooked path may seem easier at first. It may protect pride, avoid discomfort, or offer a quicker result. But what is built through compromise eventually becomes a burden. Sin promises freedom and then demands secrecy.

God calls His people into the light. Not to shame them, but to free them. What is brought honestly before Him can be cleansed, healed, and restored by His grace.

Choose the sure path. Speak truth. Make things right. Let your yes be yes and your no be no.

Integrity is not the same as image. Image asks, "How do I look?" Integrity asks, "Am I truthful before God?" One can be managed from the outside; the other must be formed in the heart.

David prayed in Psalm 139:23-24, "Search me, O God, and know my heart." That is a brave prayer because it invites God into the hidden rooms. But it is also a freeing prayer. What God brings into the light can be cleansed, corrected, and healed.

The crooked way may promise relief, but it always creates a heavier burden. Lies require maintenance. Compromise requires covering. The upright path may cost more at first, but it carries the peace of not needing darkness.

Proverbs 11:3 says, "The integrity of the upright guides them." Integrity does not only protect us from being found out; it actually becomes a guide. A truthful heart often knows the next right step because it is not trying to manage a false version of reality.

If you have stepped onto a crooked path, the invitation is not to keep walking until it collapses. Come into the light now. Confession may feel costly, but hidden sin becomes costlier the longer it is protected.

Integrity may cost you an advantage, but it protects something more valuable than advantage: a clean conscience before God. Paul said in Acts 24:16, "I always take pains to have a clear conscience toward both God and man." That kind of peace is worth more than what compromise can gain.

There is peace in walking uprightly before the Lord, even when it costs you.
