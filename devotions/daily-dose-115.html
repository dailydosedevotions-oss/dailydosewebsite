Daily Dose #115

August 17, 2026

LIFE IS A VAPOUR

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 17, 2026.

PSALM 39:4-5 (ESV)

"O LORD, make me know my end and what is the measure of my days... Surely all mankind stands as a mere breath!"

Psalm 39 speaks plainly about the brevity of life. We make plans, build schedules, and speak about tomorrow as if we hold it securely, but our lives are a breath before God. Here for a little time, and then gone.

That truth humbles us. It does not mean planning is wrong. Scripture also teaches diligence and stewardship. But it does mean our plans must be held beneath the will of God. We are not the owners of tomorrow.

When life is seen as a vapour, pride loses some of its grip. Arguments that once seemed worth holding become smaller. Obedience becomes more urgent. Love becomes more important. Eternity begins to weigh more than approval, comfort, or success.

This is not a hopeless message. For the believer, the shortness of life points us to the greatness of God. Our days are brief, but He is eternal. Our strength fades, but His mercy stands. Our time is limited, but what is done for Christ is not wasted.

Ask the Lord to help you live with eternity in view. Let your plans be surrendered, your heart be softened, and your life be offered back to Him.

Moses prayed about numbering our days, and James reminds us why: life is brief. Scripture is not trying to make life feel meaningless. It is showing us that life is too meaningful to live carelessly.

Because life is brief, pride becomes foolish. Holding grudges for years becomes tragic. Chasing approval as though it can save us becomes empty. But loving God, obeying His Word, and serving people in His name become deeply significant.

In Luke 12:20, Jesus tells of the rich fool who heard, "Fool! This night your soul is required of you," after he stored up earthly security while forgetting God. That warning cuts through the noise. A short life lived for Christ is not wasted. A long life lived only for self is not gain.

Peter says in 1 Peter 1:24-25, "All flesh is like grass," and "the word of the Lord remains forever." That contrast is meant to steady us. Human life is brief, human praise fades, but God's Word stands.

When this truth settles in, it can free us from living for appearances. We do not need to spend a short life pretending, competing, or proving. We can live honestly before God, invest in what lasts, and allow eternity to teach us what really matters.

This truth should not make us morbid; it should make us faithful. A mist can still shine in the morning light. A short life can still carry eternal fruit when it is surrendered to Christ. The question is not whether our lives are brief, but whether they are offered.

The vapour matters when it belongs to God.
