Daily Dose #139

September 10, 2026

STRENGTHEN WHAT REMAINS

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on September 10, 2026.

REVELATION 3:2 (ESV)

"Wake up, and strengthen what remains and is about to die."

The words of Jesus to the church in Sardis are sobering: be watchful, and strengthen what remains. They had a name that they were alive, but something within was dying.

This is a mercy-filled warning. Jesus does not expose spiritual decline to destroy His people. He exposes it so they can wake up and return before what remains is lost.

There may be places in your life that are not dead, but weak. Prayer that has become rare. Love that has grown cold. Conviction that has become quiet. Obedience that has been delayed. Strengthen what remains.

Do not ignore the warning signs because things still look fine outwardly. God sees beneath appearance. He knows whether our hearts are burning or fading.

The good news is that Jesus calls before the lamp goes out. There is still grace to repent, rebuild, and return. A bruised reed He will not break, and smoking flax He will not quench.

Revelation 3 is serious because Jesus is speaking to His church. They had a reputation for being alive, but He saw what others could not. That means we should care more about spiritual reality than spiritual appearance.

The Lord's warning is also mercy. He tells them to wake up because there is still something to strengthen. He does not crush the weak place; He calls it back to life. Isaiah 42:3 says, "a bruised reed he will not break, and a faintly burning wick he will not quench."

If there is still conviction, still desire, still a small flame of hunger for God, do not despise it. Bring it to Christ. Ask Him to breathe on what remains and teach you how to rebuild the places that have been neglected.

Sometimes what remains is small, but small is not nothing. A weak prayer is still prayer. A faint desire for God is still something to bring to Him. A conviction that will not leave you alone may be mercy knocking on the door.

Do not wait until everything feels strong before you return. Return weak. Return honestly. Return with the little that remains, and ask the Lord to strengthen it. He is able to restore what neglect has weakened.

Strengthening what remains may look practical: rebuilding a rhythm of prayer, asking for accountability, returning to church fellowship, confessing sin, or opening Scripture even before desire feels strong. Obedience often comes before emotion catches up.

Start where you are, and let the Lord rebuild what has become fragile.

Bring Him what remains. Let Him breathe life where things have grown weak.
