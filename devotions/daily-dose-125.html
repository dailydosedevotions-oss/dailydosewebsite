Daily Dose #125

August 27, 2026

LIVING FAITH

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 27, 2026.

TITUS 3:8 (ESV)

"Those who have believed in God may be careful to devote themselves to good works."

Faith is more than words we say. Scripture is clear that those who have believed in God should be careful to devote themselves to good works. We are not saved by effort, but living faith bears evidence.

If we say we trust God but refuse to obey Him, something is wrong. If we speak of love but close our hearts to people in need, our words are being tested. Real faith moves.

This is not a call to perform for people. It is a call to let belief become obedience. Faith forgives because Christ has forgiven. Faith gives because God has been generous. Faith repents because the Spirit convicts. Faith serves because love has taken root.

The fruit may not always look dramatic. Sometimes faith looks like making the phone call, apologising, helping quietly, resisting temptation, or doing the right thing when it would be easier not to.

Ask the Lord to make your faith alive in the ordinary places of your day. Let what you believe about Jesus shape how you speak, choose, love, and respond.

1 John 3:18 says, "let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth." Compassion that never moves toward action is being exposed. The gospel produces love that becomes visible.

This does not mean every need belongs personally to us to solve. We are not God. But it does mean we should not hide behind spiritual language to avoid simple obedience. Sometimes the Lord places a need close enough for us to respond.

Jesus taught that a good tree bears good fruit. Works do not become the root of our salvation, but they do become fruit of a living faith. Grace that saves us also trains us to live differently.

Titus 2:11-12 says "the grace of God has appeared," and that grace trains us "to live self-controlled, upright, and godly lives." Grace is not passive. It does not leave us unchanged while telling us nice things about ourselves. Grace saves, teaches, corrects, and empowers obedience.

That is why this devotion is not asking for empty busyness. It is asking whether faith has reached the hands, the mouth, the schedule, the wallet, and the way we treat people. Living faith touches real life.

The early church was marked by practical care. They broke bread, shared needs, prayed, taught, and lived as people whose faith had entered daily life. Christianity was never meant to remain only in the mind. It becomes visible in love.

The point is not pressure to impress people, but surrender that obeys God.

Faith is not proven by noise. It is revealed in surrender.
