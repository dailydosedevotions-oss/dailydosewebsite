Daily Dose #13

A CHANGE OF DIRECTION

May 3, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

LUKE 15:17–20 (ESV)

“But when he came to himself, he said, ‘How many of my father’s hired servants have more than enough bread, but I perish here with hunger! I will arise and go to my father…’ And he arose and came to his father. But while he was still a long way off, his father saw him and felt compassion, and ran and embraced him and kissed him.”

There are moments in life where something becomes clear. Not perfectly, not all at once, but enough to recognise that the direction you are going in is not leading where you thought it would. It can come through a situation that didn’t turn out the way you expected, through consequences you didn’t plan for, or through a quiet awareness that something is not right.

It is not always obvious to others, but internally there is a shift. A recognition that continuing as you are will only take you further away from where you need to be.

LUKE 15:17–20 (ESV) captures that moment with the words, “he came to himself.” That is where change begins. Not with everything fixed, not with a full plan, but with clarity. A willingness to face where you are honestly.

That can be uncomfortable. It requires letting go of excuses, stepping out of denial, and acknowledging that something needs to change. But without that moment, direction never shifts. A person can stay moving, stay busy, and still remain stuck.

Change of direction does not start outwardly.

It starts inwardly.

There is often a tension at that point. A pull between staying where things are familiar and stepping into what is unknown. Familiarity can feel easier, even when it is not right. But clarity brings responsibility. Once you see it, you cannot unsee it.

The decision then becomes simple, even if it is not easy.

Stay where you are, or begin to move.

Jesus tells this story to reveal what the Father is like, not just what the son did.

LUKE 15:20 (ESV) shows the heart behind it, “while he was still a long way off… his father saw him… and ran and embraced him.”

That changes everything. The return is not met with hesitation, distance, or rejection. It is met with compassion. Before the son could explain himself, before he could fix anything, he was received.

That is what makes the step possible.

A change of direction is not about getting everything right before you move.

It is about moving toward the One who meets you as you are.

There may still be things to work through. There may still be consequences to face. But the direction has changed, and that changes everything that follows.

You do not need to have it all figured out to take the next step.

You only need to be willing to turn.

And when you do, you are not walking toward uncertainty alone.

You are stepping into the grace that has already been waiting for you.
