Daily Dose #31

WHAT YOU’RE REALLY THIRSTING FOR

May 21, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

JOHN 4:13–14 (ESV)

“Jesus said to her, ‘Everyone who drinks of this water will be thirsty again, but whoever drinks of the water that I will give him will never be thirsty again. The water that I will give him will become in him a spring of water welling up to eternal life.’”

There are things people reach for believing they will satisfy what feels empty inside. Sometimes it is success, relationships, distractions, comfort, approval, or old habits that promise relief for a moment. The problem is not always that these things exist, it is that they were never designed to carry the weight of what the soul is actually searching for.

Jesus speaks directly into that reality in JOHN 4:13–14 (ESV) when He says, “Everyone who drinks of this water will be thirsty again…” Anything outside of Him will eventually leave a person searching again. It may satisfy temporarily, it may numb something for a while, but it cannot permanently fill what only God can restore.

This is why people can keep returning to the same things over and over again and still feel empty underneath it all. The issue is often deeper than behaviour alone. Beneath the surface there is a thirst that cannot be satisfied by temporary things, no matter how many times someone goes back to them.

Jesus does not simply point out the problem, He offers the answer.

He says, “whoever drinks of the water that I will give him will never be thirsty again.” What Christ gives is not temporary relief. It is life that reaches into the deepest parts of a person and begins changing them from within. He is not offering something shallow or short-lived. He is offering fullness found in relationship with Him.

And it goes even deeper, because Jesus says, “The water that I will give him will become in him a spring of water welling up to eternal life.” What He gives does not run dry. It continues producing life within you. Peace, truth, hope, and purpose begin flowing from a source that is no longer dependent on circumstances or temporary satisfaction.

This is why returning to old things never truly works. They may promise comfort, escape, or control, but eventually they leave the same emptiness behind. Only Jesus reaches the place within a person that nothing else can fully touch.

So this becomes the real question: what are you actually thirsty for?

Because if you keep drinking from places that cannot sustain you, you will continue searching for something more. But when you come to Christ honestly and allow Him to fill what is empty, you begin to discover a life that is no longer built on constantly chasing temporary things for satisfaction.

Because what your soul truly needs…

is not something this world can manufacture.

It is found in Jesus Christ alone, and what He gives does not fade, run dry, or leave you empty again, because the life He places within you continues to flow long after everything else has failed.
