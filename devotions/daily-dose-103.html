Daily Dose #103

August 5, 2026

MERCY OVER JUDGMENT

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 5, 2026.

JAMES 2:13 (ESV)

“For judgment is without mercy to one who has shown no mercy. Mercy triumphs over judgment.”

One of the greatest burdens a person can carry is an unforgiving heart. Many people live with bitterness for years, believing they are punishing the person who hurt them, when in reality they are only imprisoning themselves. Resentment slowly drains our joy, steals our peace, and keeps old wounds alive long after the moment has passed. The person who caused the hurt may have moved on, but the one refusing to forgive continues carrying the weight every day.

This is why James reminds us that “Mercy triumphs over judgment.” As followers of Christ, we have received mercy that we could never deserve. God did not deal with us according to our sins but according to His grace. If we have experienced such mercy ourselves, we are called to extend that same mercy to others.

This does not mean ignoring sin or pretending that wrong actions do not matter. It means refusing to allow bitterness to take root in our hearts. Forgiveness sets us free from carrying a burden that Christ never intended us to bear. As we pray for those who have hurt us, God begins changing something within us. Compassion slowly replaces resentment, and healing begins where bitterness once lived.

One of the hardest lessons in life is accepting that we cannot change another person's heart. We cannot force someone to apologise, to understand our pain, or to make things right. The only heart we are responsible for before God is our own. When we surrender our hurt to Christ, we allow Him to do the work of healing within us while trusting Him to deal justly with everyone else.

How often do we continue identifying people by the worst thing they have ever done— Yet that is not how God has treated us. Through Christ, we have been shown mercy instead of condemnation. We have been offered grace instead of the judgment we deserved. If God can transform our lives, then we should never lose hope that He can work in the lives of others as well.

Today, choose mercy over bitterness.

Choose compassion over resentment.

Choose to trust God's justice instead of carrying the weight yourself.

Mercy does not make you weak.

It reminds you that the grace which changed your life is powerful enough to change someone else's as well.
