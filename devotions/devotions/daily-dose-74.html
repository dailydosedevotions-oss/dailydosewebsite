Daily Dose #74

July 07, 2026

A CHOSEN PEOPLE

1 PETER 2:9–10 (ESV)

“But you are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession, that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness into his marvelous light. Once you were not a people, but now you are God's people; once you had not received mercy, but now you have received mercy.”

Identity is one of the most important questions a person can answer. The world constantly tries to define people by their past, their achievements, their failures, their status, or the opinions of others. Yet Peter points believers to something far greater. He reminds them of who they are because of what God has done.

Peter says believers are “a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession.” These are not titles earned through personal effort. They are gifts of God's grace. Before coming to Christ, believers were separated from God by sin. Yet through Jesus, they have been brought into the family of God and given a new identity.

This truth would have been especially powerful for Peter's readers. Many were facing opposition, rejection, and difficulty because of their faith. In the middle of those struggles, Peter reminded them that their identity was not determined by what others thought about them. Their identity was determined by what God had declared about them.

The same is true today. People often spend years searching for significance, acceptance, and purpose. Many look for these things in relationships, careers, possessions, or accomplishments. While those things may have value, none of them can provide the lasting identity that is found in Christ.

Peter says that God has called His people “out of darkness into his marvelous light.” This is more than a change in circumstances. It is a transformation of life. Those who trust in Christ have been rescued from the power of sin and brought into a new relationship with God. The old life no longer defines them because they now belong to Him.

This is why 2 CORINTHIANS 5:17 (ESV) says, “Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come.” The Gospel does not simply improve people. It makes them new.

Peter also reminds believers that they have been chosen for a purpose. They are to “proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness.” God does not save people merely so they can receive His blessings. He saves them so their lives will point others toward Him. Every believer is called to reflect His character, share His truth, and live in a way that brings Him glory.

The final verse highlights the mercy of God. Peter writes, “Once you were not a people, but now you are God's people; once you had not received mercy, but now you have received mercy.” The Christian life begins and continues because of God's mercy. None of us earn our place in His family. We are welcomed because of His grace through Jesus Christ.

When the world tries to define you by your past, remember what God says about you. When you are tempted to find your worth in temporary things, remember who you belong to. When doubts arise about your value or purpose, return to the truth of God's Word.

You are not defined by what you have done.

You are not defined by what others think.

If you belong to Christ, you are part of God's people, called out of darkness and into His marvellous light.
