Daily Dose #63

June 26, 2026

THE SHELTER OF THE MOST HIGH

PSALM 91:1–2 (ESV)

“He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will abide in the shadow of the Almighty. I will say to the LORD, ‘My refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust.’”

One of the most beautiful pictures in Scripture is found in Psalm 91. The psalmist speaks about dwelling in the shelter of the Most High and resting in the shadow of the Almighty. This is not the picture of someone occasionally visiting God when life becomes difficult. It is the picture of someone who remains close to Him, lives with Him, and finds their security in His presence.

A mother hen will often gather her chicks beneath her wings when danger approaches. The chicks do not need to understand every threat around them. They simply stay close to the one who protects them. In many ways, this is the picture Psalm 91 gives us. God invites His people to remain near Him, trusting that His care is greater than the dangers, fears, and uncertainties they face.

Many people spend their lives searching for rest. They look for it in success, relationships, possessions, distractions, or achievements. Yet real rest is not found in having complete control over life. Real rest is found in knowing the One who is in control.

The challenge for many of us is that we often try to carry burdens that God never asked us to carry. We attempt to solve every problem, control every outcome, and manage every uncertainty through our own strength. Eventually the weight becomes too much. Psalm 91 reminds us that we were never designed to live that way.

God does not call us to find shelter in ourselves. He calls us to find shelter in Him.

Living in the shelter of the Most High requires intentionality. It means spending time in His Word even when life feels busy. It means listening for His voice when other voices are competing for our attention. It means choosing trust when fear feels more natural. It means remaining close to Him not only during difficult seasons but during ordinary days as well.

The promise of God's presence does not mean we will never face hardship. It means we do not face hardship alone. The shadow of the Almighty is not a place of distance but a place of nearness. It is where we discover that God's strength is greater than our weakness and His faithfulness is greater than our fears.

When life feels uncertain, the answer is not to run further ahead of God. The answer is often to draw closer. To slow down. To listen. To remain under His care. To remember that He is still our refuge, still our fortress, and still worthy of our trust.

There is a difference between knowing about God and dwelling with God. Psalm 91 calls us beyond information into relationship. It invites us to stay close enough to experience His peace, His guidance, and His protection.

Today, resist the temptation to carry everything on your own. Draw near to the Lord. Spend time in His presence. Listen to His Word. Trust what He says above what fear says.

The safest place for a believer has never been a set of circumstances.

It has always been in the presence of God.
