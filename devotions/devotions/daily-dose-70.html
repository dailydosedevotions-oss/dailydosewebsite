Daily Dose #70

July 03, 2026

SEEING THE INVISIBLE

HEBREWS 11:27 (ESV)

“By faith he left Egypt, not being afraid of the anger of the king, for he endured as seeing him who is invisible.”

One of the greatest challenges in the Christian life is learning to trust what we cannot see. We live in a world that constantly teaches us to rely on what is visible. We trust what we can touch, measure, explain, and understand. Yet faith calls us to live differently. Faith calls us to trust the God who is unseen but undeniably present.

Love provides a simple example of this truth. You cannot physically see love. You cannot hold it in your hands or place it under a microscope. Yet nobody doubts that it exists because its effects are evident. We see love demonstrated through sacrifice, kindness, patience, and care. In the same way, although we cannot physically see God, we can see the evidence of His presence, His power, and His work all around us.

The writer of Hebrews points to Moses as an example of this kind of faith. Moses walked away from the security and privilege of Egypt because he had fixed his eyes on something greater. He endured hardship, opposition, and uncertainty because he lived with the awareness that God was with him. Hebrews tells us that he endured because he was “seeing him who is invisible.”

Faith is not pretending something is true when it is not. Faith is trusting that God is who He says He is, even when we cannot see the whole picture. This is why HEBREWS 11:1 (ESV) says, “Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen.” Faith does not remove questions, challenges, or difficulties. Rather, faith allows us to continue walking with God through those things because we trust His character more than our circumstances.

There are moments in every believer's life when God seems silent, when answers do not come as quickly as we hoped, or when the road ahead feels uncertain. During those seasons, the temptation is to focus only on what is visible. We see the problem, the obstacle, the disappointment, or the fear. Yet faith lifts our eyes beyond what is immediately in front of us and reminds us that God is still at work.

The reality is that some of God's greatest work happens where human eyes cannot see. He works in hearts before circumstances change. He develops character before opportunities appear. He strengthens faith before breakthroughs arrive. Just because we cannot see Him working does not mean He has stopped working.

This truth is echoed in 2 CORINTHIANS 5:7 (ESV), which says, “For we walk by faith, not by sight.” The Christian life was never intended to be governed solely by what we can see. It is meant to be lived in confident trust in the God who sees everything.

Moses endured because his focus was fixed on God rather than on Pharaoh. His confidence was rooted in God's faithfulness rather than in his circumstances. The same invitation is extended to us today. We can either allow visible circumstances to determine our outlook, or we can allow God's promises to shape our perspective.

Faith does not mean ignoring reality. Faith means recognising that God's reality is greater than the circumstances we face.

There may be situations in your life right now where you cannot see what God is doing. You may not see the answer, the provision, the healing, or the breakthrough. Yet that does not mean God is absent. The God who was with Moses is with you. The God who was faithful then remains faithful now.

Keep trusting Him. Keep following Him. Keep looking beyond what is visible. The eyes of faith often see what natural eyes cannot, and those who learn to trust the unseen God will discover that He has been present all along.
