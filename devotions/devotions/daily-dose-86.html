Daily Dose #86

July 19, 2026

GOD'S PLAN IS GREATER THAN YOUR PAST

GENESIS 50:20 (ESV)

“As for you, you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good, to bring it about that many people should be kept alive, as they are today.”

One of the enemy's favourite tactics is to convince people that their past has disqualified them from God's purposes. Many believers carry the weight of old failures, regrets, poor decisions, and missed opportunities. They know God has forgiven them, yet they still struggle to believe that He can use them.

Joseph's story tells a different story.

When Joseph spoke these words in Genesis 50, he was looking back over years of betrayal, false accusations, hardship, and suffering. His brothers had sold him into slavery. He had been wrongly imprisoned. He had experienced disappointment after disappointment. Yet when Joseph looked back, he saw something remarkable. God had been working through every part of the story.

Joseph did not call evil good. He clearly acknowledged what had happened. “You meant evil against me.” The wrong was real. The pain was real. The injustice was real. Yet that was not the end of the story. Joseph also recognised that God was greater than the actions of people. What others intended for harm, God used for good.

This does not mean everything that happens is good. It means God is able to redeem situations that seem beyond redemption. He is able to bring purpose out of pain, wisdom out of failure, and testimony out of brokenness.

Throughout Scripture we see this pattern repeatedly. Moses was a murderer before God used him to lead Israel. David committed serious sin yet remained a man after God's own heart. Peter denied Jesus three times and later became one of the boldest leaders in the early church. The common thread is not that these people were perfect. The common thread is that God was not finished with them.

This is why ROMANS 8:28 (ESV) says, “And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are called according to his purpose.” Paul does not say all things are good. He says God works through all things for the good of those who belong to Him.

Many people spend so much time looking backwards that they struggle to move forward. They become trapped by guilt, shame, or regret. Yet when God forgives, He does not intend for His people to remain imprisoned by what Christ has already paid for.

The cross stands as the ultimate reminder that God specialises in redemption. What looked like defeat became victory. What looked like the end became the beginning. If God can bring salvation through a crucifixion, He can certainly bring purpose through the broken pieces of our lives.

This does not mean we ignore the past. There are lessons to learn, wounds to heal, and consequences that sometimes remain. But the past was never meant to become our identity. Our identity is found in Christ, and our future is found in His hands.

Joseph eventually understood that God's plan had been larger than any single chapter of his story. The same is often true for us. We may not understand everything God is doing today, but we can trust that He is still at work.

Your mistakes are not greater than God's grace.

Your failures are not greater than God's mercy.

And your past is not greater than God's purpose.

The God who redeemed Joseph's story is still redeeming stories today.
