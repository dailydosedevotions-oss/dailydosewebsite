Daily Dose #66

June 29, 2026

LET THE SHEPHERD LEAD

PSALM 23:1–6 (ESV)

“The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want. He makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters. He restores my soul. He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake. Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me; your rod and your staff, they comfort me. You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies; you anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows. Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I shall dwell in the house of the LORD forever.”

One of the hardest things for people to do is surrender control.

We like to know where we are going. We like to understand what God is doing. We like to have a plan, a timeline, and some certainty about what comes next. Yet Psalm 23 begins with a powerful reminder that the Christian life was never designed to be led by us.

David writes, “The LORD is my shepherd.”

A shepherd leads the flock.

A shepherd protects the flock.

A shepherd guides the flock.

A shepherd sees dangers that the sheep cannot see.

The sheep's responsibility is not to lead the shepherd. The sheep's responsibility is to follow.

Many of the struggles we experience come when we try to take control of areas that belong to God. We run ahead of Him, make decisions without Him, or try to force outcomes in our own strength. Then we wonder why we feel exhausted, anxious, or restless.

Psalm 23 invites us back to a place of trust.

David says, “He makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters. He restores my soul.” Notice who is doing the leading. Notice who is doing the restoring. Notice who is doing the providing.

God never asked us to carry the weight of being our own shepherd.

He asks us to trust Him enough to follow.

Many people spend their lives trying to draw their own picture of how life should look. They chase their own plans, pursue their own direction, and follow whatever seems right in the moment. Yet God created us in His image and desires to shape us according to His purpose. The Christian life is not about us creating ourselves. It is about allowing God to form Christ within us.

That requires surrender.

It requires obedience.

It requires trust.

Sometimes the Shepherd leads us through places we would not choose ourselves. There are seasons of waiting, seasons of uncertainty, and seasons where obstacles seem to appear at every turn. Yet even then, the Shepherd has not lost sight of us.

David writes, “He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake.” The Shepherd never leads aimlessly. Every step has purpose. Even when we do not understand the journey, we can trust the One who does.

Jesus revealed Himself as the fulfilment of Psalm 23 when He declared in JOHN 10:11 (ESV), “I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep.” The One leading us is not distant or indifferent. He is the Saviour who willingly gave His life for us. Every instruction He gives and every path He leads us down flows from His love.

Psalm 23 does not promise a life free from difficulty. David says, “Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me.” Notice that David walks through the valley. He does not stay there. The Shepherd remains with him every step of the way.

That is where peace is found.

Not in the absence of problems.

Not in perfect circumstances.

But in the presence of God.

The closer we stay to the Shepherd, the more we learn to recognise His voice above the noise of the world. We begin to trust His wisdom above our own understanding and His direction above our own desires.

This is why ECCLESIASTES 12:13 (ESV) says, “The end of the matter; all has been heard. Fear God and keep his commandments, for this is the whole duty of man.” At the end of the day, the call is simple. Trust God. Obey His Word. Follow where He leads.

The Shepherd sees what you cannot see.

The Shepherd knows what you do not know.

The Shepherd is already ahead of you.

So whatever you are facing today, let the Shepherd lead.

Trust His direction.

Trust His timing.

Trust His process.

The safest place a believer can be is not where life is easiest.

It is wherever the Shepherd is leading.
