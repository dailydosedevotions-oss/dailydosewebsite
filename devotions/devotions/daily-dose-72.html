Daily Dose #72

July 05, 2026

WHY DID JESUS DIE?

ISAIAH 53:4–6 (ESV)

“Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows; yet we esteemed him stricken, smitten by God, and afflicted. But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his wounds we are healed. All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned—every one—to his own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all.”

Few questions are more important than this one: Why did Jesus die?

Many people know that Jesus died on a cross, but far fewer understand why His death was necessary. The answer begins with understanding our greatest problem. Humanity's greatest problem is not a lack of education, opportunity, wealth, or success. Our greatest problem is sin. Scripture teaches that every person has fallen short of God's standard and that sin separates us from a holy God.

Isaiah saw this reality hundreds of years before Jesus was born. In Isaiah 53, he describes a suffering servant who would bear the sins of others. He writes, “All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned—every one—to his own way.” This is the story of every human heart. Left to ourselves, we wander from God. We choose our own path. We seek our own way. Yet God, in His love, made a way for sinners to be reconciled to Him.

Isaiah declares that “he was pierced for our transgressions” and “crushed for our iniquities.” Jesus did not die because He deserved punishment. He died because we did. The punishment that should have fallen upon us was placed upon Him. The sinless Son of God took the place of sinful people so that forgiveness could be offered to all who trust in Him.

This is why ROMANS 5:8 (ESV) says, “but God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.” Jesus did not wait for us to clean ourselves up before He loved us. He came for people who were broken, lost, and unable to save themselves.

The cross reveals both the seriousness of sin and the greatness of God's love. Sin is so serious that it required the death of Christ. Yet God's love is so great that He was willing to provide the sacrifice Himself. At the cross, justice and mercy meet. God's holiness is upheld, and sinners are offered forgiveness through Jesus Christ.

The story does not end with death. Jesus rose again, defeating sin and death and proving that His sacrifice was accepted by the Father. Through His death and resurrection, a way was opened for people to be restored to God.

This is why JOHN 3:16 (ESV) says, “For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life.” Eternal life is not earned through good works or religious effort. It is received through faith in Jesus Christ.

The question is not only why Jesus died. The question is what we will do with what His death accomplished. Every person must decide what they will do with Christ. The invitation of the Gospel remains the same today as it has always been: repent, believe, and trust in the One who gave His life for you.

Jesus died so that sinners could be forgiven, reconciled to God, and given new life. That is not simply good news.

It is the greatest news the world has ever heard.
