July 17, 2026

GOD WITH US — PART 2 — LET THE SHEPHERD LEAD

PSALM 23:1–6 (ESV)

“The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want. He makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters. He restores my soul. He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake. Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me; your rod and your staff, they comfort me.”

In the previous devotion, we looked at the importance of dwelling in the shelter of the Most High. Before God guides His people, He first invites them into His presence. Psalm 23 builds on that truth by showing us what happens when we allow the Shepherd to lead.

David begins by saying, “The LORD is my shepherd.” This is more than a description of God. It is a declaration of trust. A sheep depends entirely on its shepherd for protection, provision, and direction. Left alone, sheep are vulnerable and easily led astray. In the same way, God never intended His people to navigate life independently from Him.

One of the most comforting truths in this Psalm is that the Shepherd leads. David says, “He makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters. He restores my soul.” God knows what His people need even when they do not. There are times when we are tempted to rush ahead, force our own plans, or take control of situations ourselves. Yet the Shepherd sees what we cannot see and understands what we do not understand.

This is why PROVERBS 3:5–6 (ESV) says, “Trust in the LORD with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make straight your paths.” Trusting God often means following His direction before we can see where the path leads.

David also reminds us that the Shepherd's leading is not limited to comfortable places. He writes, “Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me.” Notice that the Shepherd does not abandon His sheep in the valley. His presence remains. The comfort is not found in the absence of difficulty but in the presence of God within the difficulty.

Many believers spend much of their lives asking God to reveal the entire journey. Yet God often provides enough light for the next step rather than the entire path. Following the Shepherd requires faith. It requires trusting that His direction is better than our own and believing that His plans are ultimately for our good.

The Psalm goes on to say, “You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies.” God does not always remove every challenge immediately, but He demonstrates His faithfulness in the middle of them. He provides strength when we are weak, peace when we are anxious, and hope when circumstances seem uncertain.

This same Shepherd is revealed in the New Testament when Jesus declares in JOHN 10:11 (ESV), “I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep.” Jesus is not a distant guide giving directions from afar. He is the Shepherd who willingly gave His life so that His people could have eternal life.

One of the greatest struggles in the Christian life is surrendering control. We often want to lead while asking God to follow our plans. Yet Psalm 23 reminds us that peace is found when we trust the Shepherd enough to let Him lead.

The closer we walk with Him, the more we discover that His direction is trustworthy, His provision is sufficient, and His presence is constant.

The Shepherd knows the way.

Our responsibility is to follow Him.
