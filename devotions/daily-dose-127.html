Daily Dose #127

August 29, 2026

FRUIT WILL SHOW

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on August 29, 2026.

MATTHEW 12:33 (ESV)

"Either make the tree good and its fruit good, or make the tree bad and its fruit bad, for the tree is known by its fruit."

Jesus teaches that fruit reveals the tree. Words matter, but fruit tells the truth over time. A life rooted in Christ will begin to show evidence of His work within.

This is not about judging others harshly while ignoring our own hearts. Jesus also warned about noticing the speck in another's eye while missing the beam in our own. The first place to examine fruit is before the Lord personally.

What is growing in you? Is there humility, repentance, love, patience, purity, mercy, and obedience? Or has bitterness, pride, compromise, and selfishness been allowed to take root?

Fruit grows from abiding. Jesus said in John 15:5, "apart from me you can do nothing." We cannot produce spiritual fruit by forcing ourselves to look better. We bear fruit by staying connected to Christ, receiving His Word, and yielding to the Holy Spirit.

If the Lord exposes unhealthy fruit, do not run from Him. Let conviction become an invitation to repentance and renewal. The Gardener knows how to prune what He loves.

Galatians 5:22-23 says "the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control." These are not decorations for unusually spiritual people. They are the evidence of the Spirit's work in a surrendered life.

Fruit also takes time. A tree does not bear mature fruit overnight, but it should be alive and growing. If the Lord shows you fruit that is unhealthy, do not turn that conviction into despair. Bring it to the Gardener.

That keeps us humble. We do not produce lasting fruit by trying to look better. We bear fruit by abiding in Him, receiving His Word, and allowing His Spirit to shape us from within.

This is a good place for honest examination, not fear-filled self-hatred. The Spirit convicts as a Helper, not an accuser. He shows us what is unhealthy so we can bring it to Jesus and be changed.

Fruit also proves the health of the root. If the visible fruit is consistently anger, pride, impurity, envy, or division, the answer is not to tape better fruit onto the branches. The answer is to return to the root: abiding in Christ.

The Lord is patient with growth, but He is not indifferent to fruitlessness. That balance matters. We should neither despair over slow growth nor excuse a life that refuses to change. Grace is patient, and grace is powerful.

Healthy fruit may begin quietly, but over time it will testify that Christ is at work.

Fruit will show. Stay rooted in Jesus, and let Him make your life fruitful for His glory.
