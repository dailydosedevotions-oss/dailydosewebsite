Daily Dose #146

September 17, 2026

THE BLESSED HOPE

This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on September 17, 2026.

TITUS 2:13 (ESV)

"Waiting for our blessed hope, the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ."

The Christian life looks forward. We are not only trying to survive today; we are looking for the blessed hope, the glorious appearing of Jesus Christ.

This hope is not wishful thinking. It is anchored in the promise of God. The One who came in humility will come again in glory. The rejected King will be seen as Lord of all.

Remembering His return changes how we live now. It teaches us to say no to ungodliness and worldly lusts, as the surrounding verses in Titus explain. Hope does not make us careless. It makes us holy.

When the world feels heavy, lift your eyes. When obedience feels costly, lift your eyes. When sorrow feels long, lift your eyes. The story does not end with darkness, confusion, or death.

Jesus is coming. That truth comforts the weary and sobers the careless. It calls us to live ready, faithful, and full of expectation.

Titus connects this hope with training in godliness. The grace of God does not only forgive; it trains us to renounce ungodliness and worldly passions. Waiting for Jesus should make us cleaner, not careless.

The return of Christ comforts the weary and sobers the distracted. If He is coming, then suffering is not forever. If He is coming, then compromise is not worth it. If He is coming, then faithfulness in hidden places matters more than we can see.

This hope is called blessed because it is not fragile. It does not depend on the headlines, the economy, the approval of people, or the strength of our circumstances. It rests on the promise of the risen Lord.

Romans 8:22-23 says "the whole creation has been groaning together," and that believers also "groan inwardly as we wait eagerly for adoption as sons, the redemption of our bodies." We feel that groaning too. We see sickness, grief, injustice, and death, and something in us knows this is not how things are meant to remain. The blessed hope answers that ache with the promise of Christ.

Hope also gives endurance. 2 Corinthians 4:17-18 reminds us that "this light momentary affliction is preparing for us an eternal weight of glory beyond all comparison," as we look to "the things that are unseen." If our Lord endured with His eyes fixed beyond the suffering, then His people can endure with their eyes fixed on Him and on the glory He has promised.

The blessed hope also teaches us patience. We do not yet see everything restored, but we know the Restorer is coming. That hope gives courage to keep obeying when the world seems upside down and faithfulness feels costly.

The coming King gives today's obedience eternal meaning.

Let the blessed hope steady your heart today. The King will come.
