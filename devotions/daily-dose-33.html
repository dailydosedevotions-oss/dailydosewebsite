Daily Dose #33

DON’T MISS YOUR MOMENT (PART 2)

May 23, 2026
This devotion is scheduled and will become available at 7am on its release date.

2 KINGS 5:1–8, 15 (ESV)

“Naaman, commander of the army of the king of Syria, was a great man with his master and in high favor, because by him the Lord had given victory to Syria. He was a mighty man of valor, but he was a leper. Now the Syrians on one of their raids had carried off a little girl from the land of Israel, and she worked in the service of Naaman’s wife. She said to her mistress, ‘Would that my lord were with the prophet who is in Samaria! He would cure him of his leprosy.’ So Naaman went in and told his lord, ‘Thus and so spoke the girl from the land of Israel.’ And the king of Syria said, ‘Go now, and I will send a letter to the king of Israel.’ So he went, taking with him ten talents of silver, six thousand shekels of gold, and ten changes of clothing. And he brought the letter to the king of Israel, which read, ‘When this letter reaches you, know that I have sent to you Naaman my servant, that you may cure him of his leprosy.’ And when the king of Israel read the letter, he tore his clothes and said, ‘Am I God, to kill and to make alive, that this man sends word to me to cure a man of his leprosy? Only consider, and see how he is seeking a quarrel with me.’ But when Elisha the man of God heard that the king of Israel had torn his clothes, he sent to the king, saying, ‘Why have you torn your clothes? Let him come now to me, that he may know that there is a prophet in Israel.’… Then he returned to the man of God, he and all his company, and he came and stood before him. And he said, ‘Behold, I know that there is no God in all the earth but in Israel.’”

Naaman’s story is a clear picture of what was spoken in Part 1, because he is in a moment that has the power to change everything, yet the way he responds to it determines whether he receives what God is doing.

He has position, he has success, and he has recognition, but he still has a need that nothing around him can fix. And that is the reality for many people, because you can have everything externally and still carry something internally that has not been healed.

The moment comes through something unexpected, through a servant girl speaking truth. It is not loud, it is not dramatic, but it is direct. And this is exactly what you see in ISAIAH 55:6 (ESV) — “Seek the Lord while he may be found…” because the moment is there, the opportunity is there, but it still requires a response.

Naaman moves toward the moment, but the struggle begins when it does not happen the way he expects. Pride begins to rise, because he expected something different, something more fitting to his position, something that made sense to him.

This is where ISAIAH 55:8–9 (ESV) — “For my thoughts are not your thoughts…” becomes real, because God’s way did not match Naaman’s expectation. And instead of immediately receiving it, he resists it.

That is how moments are missed. Not because God is not working, but because His way does not match what we thought it would be.

Naaman had the opportunity to walk away. He could have stayed in his condition, not because there was no answer, but because his pride refused to accept it in the way it was given.

But something shifts, and when he finally responds, when he lets go of his own expectation and aligns himself with what was spoken, everything changes.

This connects directly back to ISAIAH 55:11 (ESV) — “so shall my word be… it shall not return to me empty” because the word spoken over his life was always carrying the answer. The issue was never the word, it was whether he would receive it.

And when he does respond, when he steps into alignment, the result is not just healing, but recognition. He comes to a place where he says there is no God like the God of Israel, showing that what began as a need ends in transformation.

This is what brings everything together, because the same truth from Part 1 is seen here in real life. The moment was there. The word was spoken. The opportunity was given. But the outcome was determined by the response.

So the question is not whether God is speaking into your life, but how you are responding to it. Because just like Naaman, you can be close to the answer and still walk away from it if you are not aligned.

But when you choose to let go of your own way, your own thinking, and your own expectation, and respond to what God is saying, everything begins to shift.

Because the moment you are in right now…

may not look how you expected.

But it still carries the answer you need.

And if you respond to it, align with it, and trust God in it, what He has spoken over your life will not return empty, but will accomplish exactly what He intended, not just around you, but within you.
